By Spiky Hedgehog<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Ken Weller, who died aged 85 on January 25th, was among Britain\u2019s brightest working-class militants. Co-founder of the irreverent libertarian socialist group Solidarity, he is remembered by many as a clear-thinking, hard-talking mentor. His political trajectory spanned the movement against nuclear weapons in the 1960s, industrial militancy, and the development of a vision of a self-managed society.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In autumn 1961 Ken and a handful of others occupied the Russian Embassy to protest against<\/a> the Soviet \u2018workers\u2019 bomb\u2019.  His anti-bomb activism continued in 1963 when, as one of the \u2018Spies for Peace\u2019, Ken helped expose British state secrets in which an elite planned to govern in the wake of a nuclear attack. These revelations provoked a public crisis of confidence in the British state. A manhunt for the \u2018spies\u2019 ensued but, despite police raiding and searching Ken\u2019s house, none of the peace activists<\/a> were ever caught. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

As an industrial militant, Ken also produced popular articles and pamphlets for Solidarity that drew on his experience as a worker and shop steward to advance the premise that challenges at work and in society at large could only be solved by the actions of workers themselves. Many of these pamphlets were popular in labour and social movement circles. Some also made national headlines.<\/p>\n\n\n
